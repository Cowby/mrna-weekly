## 🔬 Highlight of the Week

### **Beyond the Sequence: Chemical and Topological Design in mRNA Therapeutics**
**Chemical Reviews** | March 24, 2026 | DOI: 10.1021/acs.chemrev.5c00347

This comprehensive review introduces a **new dimension** in mRNA engineering: **topological design**.

**Key Innovations:**
- **Circular RNAs** - Enhanced stability without poly(A) tail degradation
- **Branched structures** - Multi-functional mRNA architectures
- **Synthetic lariat architectures** - Mimicking natural splicing intermediates

**Four Pillars of Modern mRNA Engineering:**
1. **Chemical modifications** (Ψ, m1Ψ, m5C, m6A across cap, UTRs, CDS, poly(A))
2. **Topological engineering** (linear, circular, branched)
3. **Structure-activity relationships** (translation, decay, immunogenicity)
4. **Therapeutic implications** (next-generation mRNA drugs)

**Why It Matters:**
Traditional mRNA design focuses on sequence optimization. This review shows that **3D topology** is equally important - circular mRNAs can translate continuously without degradation, branched structures can encode multiple proteins, and synthetic architectures can evade immune sensing while maintaining function.
